PNG vs JPG: Pros and Cons

Advantages of PNG

  • Allows for transparency, making it ideal for web graphics and layered designs.
  • Maintains image quality with lossless compression, even after multiple edits.
  • Perfect for images with text or sharp details due to excellent clarity.
  • Well-suited for technical diagrams and screenshots featuring fine details.
  • Compatible with most web browsers and graphic design software.

Drawbacks of PNG

  • Produces larger file sizes compared to JPG, which can slow down website performance.
  • Not the best option for storing high-resolution photographs due to its size limitations.
  • Can cause slower load times on pages that heavily rely on PNG images.
  • Has a limited color palette when compared to newer formats like WebP.
  • Lacks native animation support, unlike formats such as GIF.
